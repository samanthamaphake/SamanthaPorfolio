My Journey as a Software Developer

Published on: 5th December 2024

I began my journey in tech with simple things — learning how to write basic HTML to create a webpage. At the time, I didn’t fully understand what I was doing, but I loved the idea of building something from scratch that people could actually see and use.

As I explored more, I started learning about CSS to style my pages, and JavaScript to make them interactive. That opened up a whole new world of creativity and problem-solving. I eventually moved on to building full websites and small applications — not just the front-end (what users see), but also the back-end (how it all works behind the scenes).

One of the things that really sparked my passion was working on real-life projects. These gave me hands-on experience and helped me connect my skills to actual problems. Some of the projects I’m proud of include:

A customer loyalty prediction model that uses data to help businesses understand which customers are likely to leave — and why. An aviation safety monitoring system designed to detect signs of pilot fatigue, using a mix of research, data input, and system logic. Several interactive dashboards in Power BI, where I turned raw data into easy-to-understand visuals to support decision-making.

Through these projects, I developed not only technical skills but also personal qualities like:

  • Time management – balancing code deadlines with testing and fixing issues
  • Critical thinking – solving unexpected problems when things didn’t work
  • Teamwork – communicating with others, especially when working on group assignments or presenting results

    • What excites me most is that software development is always changing. There’s always something new to learn — whether it's a new programming language, a better design practice, or a smarter way to handle data.

    I’m currently focused on growing both as a developer and a data enthusiast, aiming to build systems that are not just functional, but also meaningful and user-friendly.
